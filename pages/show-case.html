Show Case

Here you will find work that I have done so far. All these pieces have been done for school. Each artwork created started with research to learn about the topic or style of graphics. The next step of the process is always paper sketches. Those sketches get uploaded to my computer where the magic happens to create final products!

Type Poster

This artwork was created to highlight a typeface. The typeface chosen was Rockwell Nova. This is a slab serif font created by Frank Hinman Pierpont. With this poster, the goal was to highlight something unique about the typeface. Rockwell Nova is very geometric and was designed by an engineer, therefore, the poster is centered around a blueprint theme.

Theatre Poster

This artwork was created for Ottawa Little Theatre. The poster was made to promote Terry Hatcher’s The Turn of the Screw. This story is about a haunted mansion, therefore, the poster design aired on the horror side to showcase the theme of the play. Featured on the poster is the mansion as well as a ghost to give viewers a sense of the storyline.

Alphabet

This artwork was designed as a new alphabet. The goal was to create nine symbols that looked harmonious but also were distinctly different. This was a piece created by hand. Each symbol was measured, drawn, and then filled in with black marker.

Infographic

This artwork is an infographic about chicken and egg production. Each element was drawn by hand and then remade in illustrator. The goal for this project was to produce an infographic with numerous charts and images to help viewers move along the page and process the information.

Lego Campaign

This artwork is one of six designs for a Lego ad campaign. The goal for this was to create distinctive and creative advertisements for the Lego jazz quartet set. The theme for these ads is “Music That Transports You.” The ads were meant to give viewers a sense of adventureand fun.
